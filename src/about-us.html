ABOUT JOT

Mental health journaling since 2020.

OUR MISSION

According to the 2019 State of Mental Health in America report, over 44 million American adults have a mental illness. Though therapy can help the mentally ill improve their well-being, one of the biggest obstacles in therapy is tracking mental health progress. According to Psychology Today, patients should introspect between sessions for best results. Furthermore, one of the best ways to do this is for patients to articulate their thoughts through journaling. However, patients often forego this technique, resulting in less effective treatment and an inability to track progress. That's where Jot comes in: our platform offers a space for patients to journal about their therapy experiences. This way, users can highlight important principles discussed with their therapist, track their progress quantitatively, and maintain frequent communcation with their mental health professional.

OUR PLATFORM INCLUDES:

A Journal Hub allowing users to create new entries while keeping track of previously written prompts. Notes can be shared with mental health professionals or made private.

A progress chart offering a detailed view of inputted mood over time. This way, progress can be tracked quantitatively.

A live chat connecting patients to their mental health professionals. To use this feature, your mental health professional must be registered and listed as your therapist.
